History and Introduction

Introduction

St. John Ambulance Malaysia - Selangor Darul Ehsan (SJAM-SDE) is a leading First Aid and humanitarian organisation dedicated to serving the community with excellence and compassion.

Our motto "Pro Utilitate Hominum - For the Service of Mankind" reflects our commitment to providing essential emergency services and humanitarian aid to those in need.

With over 4,422 dedicated members, SJAM-SDE operates comprehensive healthcare services including haemodialysis, emergency ambulance services, community services, and disaster relief assistance for floods, fires, landslides, and plane crashes.

We also provide extensive training programs for uniformed members and cadets, ensuring the highest standards of emergency response and first aid care.

History

The formation of St. John Council in 1972 marked the beginning of organised emergency medical services in Malaysia. This pivotal moment established the foundation for what would become a comprehensive network of first aid and humanitarian services.

In 1990, the official segregation of SJAM-SDE took place, creating a dedicated organisation specifically serving the Selangor Darul Ehsan region. This separation allowed for more focused and efficient service delivery to the local community.

Under the leadership of Dr. Chen Soo-See and Mr. Ho Thiam Hook as State Commanders, SJAM-SDE experienced significant growth and development. Their vision and dedication established the organisation as a trusted provider of emergency medical services.

Mr. Yeo Kim Thong served as Deputy State Commander before becoming State Commander, continuing the tradition of excellence. In 2000, his outstanding contributions were recognised with a prestigious award, highlighting his commitment to the organisation's mission and values.

Organisation Structure

The SJAM-SDE State HQ is the administrative and policy-making body that decides the directions of the state. These officers are active in promoting the aim of SJAM-SDE and the noble cause of the Order of St. John. Besides carrying our first aid and emergency services duties, the State/Area officers are involved in various charities and community services.
